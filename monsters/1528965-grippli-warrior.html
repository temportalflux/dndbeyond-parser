Grippli Warrior

Grippli Warrior
Small Humanoid (Grippli)
Armor Class 12
Hit Points 13 (3d6 + 3)
Speed 30 ft., climb 30 ft., swim 30 ft.
STR
10 (+0)
DEX
15 (+2)
CON
12 (+1)
INT
10 (+0)
WIS
14 (+2)
CHA
10 (+0)
Skills Athletics +2, Stealth +4, Survival +4
Senses Passive Perception 12
Languages Grippli plus one other language (usually Common, Draconic, or Primordial)
Challenge 1/4 (50 XP)
Proficiency Bonus +2

Hold Breath. The grippli can hold its breath for 20 minutes.

Standing Leap. The grippli can leap 30 feet horizontally or 20 feet vertically from a standing position.

Actions

Multiattack. The grippli makes one attack with its tongue. If this attack hits, the grippli can make a melee attack using its trident against the same target.

Tongue. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one Medium or smaller creature. Hit: The target is grappled (escape DC 12). Until this grapple ends, the target is restrained, and the grippli can‚Äôt grab another creature.

Trident. Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6 + 2) piercing damage, or 6 (1d8 + 2) piercing damage if used with two hands to make a melee attack, plus 2 (1d4) piercing damage if the grippli had advantage on the attack roll.

Shortbow. Ranged Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, range 80/320 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6 + 2) piercing damage, plus 2 (1d4) piercing damage if the grippli had advantage on the attack roll.

Description

At first glance or from a distance, a grippli looks like a large frog. Its head and body are indeed those of a frog, but its front extremities end in hands with fingers that enable it to manipulate weapons and other objects, and its rear legs have similar digits that it can use for grasping and climbing. A grippli can move as quickly on all fours as it can on two legs.

Communal Effort. Grippli rely on ambushes and guerrilla warfare to defend themselves and their settlements. They can snare foes with their tongues to make them easier targets. Grippli villages are generally built from mud bricks and other natural materials harvested from the immediate vicinity.

Young grippli are raised by the community rather than by a single set of parents, and all of a community‚Äôs eggs are kept in freshwater pools until they mature. Protecting their eggs is a major concern for grippli, since lizardfolk, yuan-ti, and other creatures are known to have a taste for them.

Close to Nature. Most grippli care more about survival than they do about abstract ideals such as good and evil. Grippli religion is based in animism and nature spirits; the frogfolk do not pay reverence to a single deity, but they might beseech a particular nature spirit for aid from time to time.

Candlekeep Mysteries, pg. 99

    Alignment gives DM's a baseline for creatures morals/ethics/code, etc. It allows us to understand if a species will abide by specific laws/rules, their own personal ones or they are more chaotic or not. If they are good or evil. It is a simple two letter code that explains far more than just those two letters. 

    Without an alignment you have no clue how a creature will react and it becomes just a pretty picture and a bunch of numbers with no understanding how it fits in to D&D. If you as a DM want to change a creatures alignment obviously you can. But knowing a troll is Chaotic Evil normally means players will be caught off guard when you introduce a Lawful or Neutral Good troll. You can work around that and subvert their expectations. 

     

    Lots of DM's love keeping creatures the way they are written in the books and seeing everything in the stat block makes it easier for them.

    I personally don't care about alignments for player characters but for NPCs it's a great short hand at a glance how the generic monster of that type would typically behave on the ethic/moral compass. Most creatures follow the same alignments of the creator gods but it's a rule of thumb than written in stone law. In fact the the MM that: "The alignment specified in a monster‚Äôs stat block is the default. Feel free to depart from it and change a monster‚Äôs alignment to suit the needs of your campaign. If you want a good-aligned green dragon or an evil storm giant, there‚Äôs nothing stopping you."

    So for Grippli Warriors, they would tend to be Chaotic Neutral for example but there's nothing stopping a DM for having individual Grippli Warriors to be Lawful Good. The CN alignment is a guideline as the default alignment (based on their culture and/or religious following).

    If you can fallen angels, such as the archdevil Zariel, you can also have risen demons. Back in 3.5E, WotC published in Fight Club a LG paladin succubus (archive.wizards.com/default.asp?x=dnd/fc/20050824a). There is nothing stopping players or DMs from using non-default alignments.

    As a player/DM since Basic/AD&D I would have no problem fleshing monsters out but this is would probably be harder for new DMs.
