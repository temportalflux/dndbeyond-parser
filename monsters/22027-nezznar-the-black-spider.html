    I just went through the encounter with Nezznar with a group that, up to that point, cake walked every encounters by outsmarting them. I commit to play Nezznar without any upgrade, but decide that I will play him as smartly as possible. He doesn't stand a chance to win in a straight fight, so here out it went : He ambushed the PC after they god rid of the spectator. He was quite glad that the PC solved the problem for him. It could have been after any of the hard encounter that Nezznar is trying to avoid. The spectator room is perfect, because it is small. Nezznar first move was Darkness, to blind the PC. The giant spiders move in, get close to PC with their blindsight, and entangle them with their web. So they fight with advantage while the PC fight with disadvantage, and blocking the doorway to keep them inside the darkness spell. Nezznar keep far back out of reach and taunt the PC while the spider eat them alive. Without the Daylight, my PC where going down real fast.  

    Other thing to take into consideration, if you don't let him get surprise by the PC, Nezznar has prepared for the battle :

    - Nezznar has mage armor + shield ready. That's 19 AC. Keep your 1st level spell for shield.

    - He have spider climb from the staff, so he can fight from the ceiling, making him to detect when ambushing and too high to reach in melee in chosen rooms (fight in those).

    - As soon as the PC get rid of Darkness, or out of it, have Nezznar drop it to cast web with the staff, the DC, at 15, is quite high and hard to break. Both are concentration, so he can't have darkness and web at the same time.

    - He has no change to win a straight fight 1 vs 4-5 PC, so if it gets to that, invisibility + flee. Bring him back later as a Dryder if your PCs didn't get their spook enough.

    After how it played out with my group, I'm convinced you don't have to upgrade him to make him a deadly opponent.
