    It is a time of revelry and celebration, where people eat, drink, and play games of chance or skill. Doing so ensures a bountiful harvest season.

    There is only one rule: to honor the goddess, no violence is permitted within the festival grounds.

    So when an NPC is killed and their body is found during the celebration, suddenly the authorities must discreetly locate the killer without causing a panic.

    Several NPC‚Äôs are suspects...but some investigation from the party uncovers one of them secretly being a cleric of the deity Loviatar, goddess of pain. This cleric animated a local scarecrow in the field to commit the murder, without being discovered.

    The party encounters the animated scarecrow while scouting the murder scene, and finds evidence of the NPC‚Äôs guilt embedded inside the scarecrow (a matching amulet, or some component of the spell which is also found on the guiltily NPC).

    This makes a good murder-mystery for an early campaign encounter.
