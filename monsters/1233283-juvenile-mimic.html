    Quote from Splunki >>

    pog FAMILIAR MIMIC pog

     

     What exactly are the rules for what can be a familiar?

     

    If you're talking about a magic familiar from the Find Familiar, it clearly states that only the following can be familiars:  bat, cat, crab, frog (toad), hawk, lizard, octopus, owl, poisonous snake, fish (quipper), rat, raven, sea horse, spider, or weasel.  Warlocks with the Pact of the Chain boon also get imp, pseudodragon, quasit, or sprite.  This means that unless your DM makes an exception, you cannot have a Juvenile Mimic from the Find Familiar spell.

    If you're talking about a regular pet, there aren't any strict rules as to what can or cannot be a non-combat pet.  There are sidekick rules in TCoE that state: "A sidekick can be any type of creature with a stat block in the Monster Manual or another D&D book, but the challenge rating in its stat block must be 1/2 or lower."  This means a juvenile mimic would be allowed as a sidekick assuming your DM allows you to somehow convince it to join you.

    There are other ways to get a familiar besides a find familiar spell. Pseudodragons, Gazers, and Anvilwrought Raptors are some examples that have printed familiar variants that you can get by making a contract with them. The big difference is they aren't immortal like the summoned familiars. 

    The key aspect of being a familiar is the magical psychic connection the master has with their familiar as opposed to, say, a pet housecat. Pseudodragons for example:

    Familiar. The pseudodragon can serve another creature as a familiar, forming a magic, telepathic bond with that willing companion. While the two are bonded, the companion can sense what the pseudodragon senses as long as they are within 1 mile of each other. While the pseudodragon is within 10 feet of its companion, the companion shares the pseudodragon‚Äôs Magic Resistance trait. At any time and for any reason, the pseudodragon can end its service as a familiar, ending the telepathic bond.
