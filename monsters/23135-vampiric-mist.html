In billowing clouds of fog lurk vampiric mists, the wretched remnants of vampires that were prevented from finding rest. Indistinguishable from the mists they lurk within, they strike unseen and undetected to bleed their victims dry.

    Quote from DarthRevan1997 >>

    So how does the life drain ability interact with a PC that has temporary HP? Does the necrotic damage hit the temp, then also drain away from the pc's max hp, or does only the temp take damage, then prevent the max hp drain since the damage did not hit the PC itself. 

     

    Temporary health does not increase a character's hit point maximum. Those hit points cannot be restored. But they are the first hit points to be lost. The necrotic damage will deplete the temporary hit points first, but that doesn't necessarily mean that the character's maximum hit points won't be reduced. If the damage from the life drain exceeds the character's temporary hit points, the remaining damage will deplete the character's normal hit points, and reduce the maximum by however much damage reaches their normal hit points.

    In other words, if a character has a normal pool of 32 hit points, and 6 temporary hit points, and receives 8 damage from the vampiric mist's life drain attack, the character will lose all 6 temporary hit points, and 2 normal hit points, and their maximum hit points will be reduced by 2 to 30.
