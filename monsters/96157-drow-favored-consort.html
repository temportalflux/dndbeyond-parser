Drow

Whether they are found in Underdark cities, the darkened passages between them, or in the dreaded Demonweb Pits, drow stand as one of the most insidious threats to surface-dwellers. Devoted to Lolth, the dark elves obey her often-contradictory commands and live in fear of her wrath. Some attain great power and influence through cunning, talent, or subterfuge, evoking fear from the lesser drow who serve them.

Drow Favored Consort

Nearly all priestesses of Lolth, including the powerful matron mothers, take attractive drow as their consorts. Often these individuals serve no purpose beyond pleasure, breeding, or both, but sometimes consorts can gain the ear of their priestess and be relied on to provide useful advice. No position of consort is assured for long; priestesses are infamous for being fickle with their favor, which are they are especially glad to lavish on a consort who combines beauty with magical might.

    Quote from ironic_dragonborne >>

    I thinkk this is the least worthy monster of CR 18. It's sitting pretty next to young blue shadowdragons, adult red dracoliches and demiliches. This this is killed by any adult dragon in 5 or so rounds. No joke, it's actually just a weaker drow inquisitor.

     So, you're determining your CR way wrong. It's not a matter of what NPC could beat what in a one-on-one fight-to-the-death. There is a big ol' table in the back of the DMG on how to determine a monster's CR based on its stats. I did the math for both this and the inquisitor, and while both were overrated (CR-wise), the consort is way better than the inquisitor. Through my math, DFC has an offensive CR of anywhere from 18-20 (depending on how you factor its spells and how it utilizes its various features). I put it at 19 because I used the Best-Case-Scenario:

    • Starts off with Invisibility for an ambush with 3 scimitar attacks
    • First round of actual combat is Haste + Extra Action atk + Bonus Action atk from War Magic
    • Second round is 4 atks (3 from action, 1 from haste)
    • Third round is Chain Lightning (to take advantage of the save-dis from the 4 scimitar attacks) with two extra attacks

    Defensively, I decided that, like any caster, Mage Armor would be cast the first thing in the morning, so 18 is its AC to start with. Haste increases that to 20, and then it also has 3 save proficiencies, which, combined with its HP, make its defensive CR 13. Overall, its CR should really be ~16.

    The Inquisitor, unfortunately, suffers from much the same problem of overestimation. Offensively, it is much less interesting, relying almost exclusively on its Multiattack (as pretty much all of its spells are single-target and deal less damage than its weapon attacks). However, it does have Spiritual Weapon, which in the interest of the best-case-scenario, I ha it cast at 6th level. All of this made its offensive CR pretty solidly 16. Defensively, it actually has a fair amount going for it. 143 HP puts it at CR 5, but Magic Resistance and its 3 save proficiencies each increase that by 1, making its overall CR ~11.

    You're right in saying that it's not a right and proper CR 18, though your rationale isn't quite right, but in no way is this worse than a Drow Inquisitor. You must not have been factoring in its spellcasting capabilities.

    ETA: Also, his spell save DC shoul be 18, not 13, and his Passive Perception should be 18, not 16.
