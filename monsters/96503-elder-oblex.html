Elder Oblex
Huge Ooze, Lawful Evil
Armor Class 16
Hit Points 115 (10d12 + 50)
Speed 20 ft.
STR
15 (+2)
DEX
16 (+3)
CON
21 (+5)
INT
22 (+6)
WIS
13 (+1)
CHA
18 (+4)
Saving Throws INT +10, CHA +8
Skills Arcana +10, Deception +8, History +10, Nature +10, Perception +5, Religion +10
Condition Immunities Blinded, Charmed, Deafened, Exhaustion, Prone
Senses Blindsight 60 ft. (blind beyond this distance), Passive Perception 15
Languages Common plus six more
Challenge 10 (5,900 XP)
Proficiency Bonus +4

Amorphous. The oblex can move through a space as narrow as 1 inch wide without squeezing.

Aversion to Fire. If the oblex takes fire damage, it has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks until the end of its next turn.

Innate Spellcasting. The oblex‚Äôs innate spellcasting ability is Intelligence (spell save DC 18). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:

At will: charm person (as 5th-level spell), detect thoughts, hold person

3/day each: confusion, dimension door, dominate person, fear, hallucinatory terrain, hold monster, hypnotic pattern, telekinesis

Sulfurous Impersonation. As a bonus action, the oblex can extrude a piece of itself that assumes the appearance of one Medium or smaller creature whose memories it has stolen. This simulacrum appears, feels, and sounds exactly like the creature it impersonates, though it smells faintly of sulfur. The oblex can impersonate 2d6 + 1 different creatures, each one tethered to its body by a strand of slime that can extend up to 120 feet away. For all practical purposes, the simulacrum is the oblex, meaning the oblex occupies its space and the simulacrum‚Äôs space simultaneously. The slimy tether is immune to damage, but it is severed if there is no opening at least 1 inch wide between the oblex‚Äôs main body and the simulacrum. The simulacrum disappears if the tether is severed.

Actions

Multiattack. The elder oblex makes two pseudopod attacks and uses Eat Memories.

Pseudopod. Melee Weapon Attack: +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 17 (4d6 + 3) bludgeoning damage plus 7 (2d6) psychic damage.

Eat Memories. The oblex targets one creature it can see within 5 feet of it. The target must succeed on a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw or take 44 (8d10) psychic damage and become memory drained until it finishes a short or long rest or until it benefits from the greater restoration or heal spell. Constructs, oozes, plants, and undead succeed on the save automatically.

While memory drained, the target must roll a d4 and subtract the number rolled from any ability check or attack roll it makes. Each time the target is memory drained beyond the first, the die size increases by one: the d4 becomes a d6, the d6 becomes a d8, and so on until the die becomes a d20, at which point the target becomes unconscious for 1 hour. The effect then ends.

When an oblex causes a target to become memory drained, the oblex learns all the languages the target knows and gains all its proficiencies, except any saving throw proficiencies.

By experimenting on the slimes, jellies, and puddings that infest the depths of the Underdark, mind flayers created a special breed of ooze, the oblex ‚Äî a slime capable of assaulting the minds of other creatures. Cunning hunters, these pools of jelly stalk prey, searching for the memories they so desperately crave. When oblexes feed on those thoughts, sometimes killing their victims, they can form weird copies of their prey, which help them to harvest even more victims for their dark masters.

Memory Eaters. Oblexes feed on thoughts and memories. The sharper the mind, the better the meal, so oblexes hunt obviously intelligent targets such as wizards and other spellcasters. When suitable fare comes within reach, an oblex draws its body up to engulf its victim. As it withdraws, it plunders the creature‚Äôs mind, leaving its prey befuddled and confused.

Ooze Nature. An oblex doesn‚Äôt require sleep.

Adult and Elder Oblexes

Newly formed oblexes lack the capabilities of their older kin. They seek only to feed on memories and grow until they can impersonate their victims.

Older oblexes, called adults and elders, have eaten so many memories that they can form duplicates of the creatures they have devoured from the substance of their bodies, sending them off to lure prey into their clutches, while remaining tethered to the slime by long tendrils of goo. These duplicated creatures are indistinguishable from their victims except for a faint sulfurous smell. Oblexes use these duplicates to lure prey into danger or to infiltrate settlements so they can feed on superior victims.

Environment: SwampUnderdarkUrban

    I believe you are getting Mass & Volume confused with Area.

    The Mass (aka Density) can be reduced in order to increase Volume (aka Substance) in order to cover a larger Area (aka Space).

    It makes LESS sense for this creature to be Huge, since it would be keeping the Memories in a localized area, meanwhile the Gelatinous portions are being stretched & twisted in order to affect & interact with their environment.

    You are mentally assuming (likely unconsciously) that each Simulacrum is in fact whole, when in actuality it only needs its shell & communicative bits, none of the internals are required as the Simulacrum is effectively a Pseudopod on its own, thus it retains the Strength & Constitution Score of the Core, & with a proper Vocal Cord build it is capable of effectively recreating Speech accurately.

    I believe the confusion when this Stat Block was built was that the Make-a-Wish Foundation Kid was stating that when they became large enough (effectively Huge) they would create an Oblex Spawn, which happens when they absorb a 14th person's complete Memories. Otherwise, Elder Oblex by all intents and purposes should be a Large, not a Huge, and the Statistical Evidence supports this claim.

    2. It pushes only enough Mass to recreate the Exterior & Vocal components, the internals are vacuous, empty space, trapped gasses stored for the sole purpose of "Speech"
    3. If all of the Mass was collected, it would be enough to create at Most 13 Impersonations, again you incorrectly stated this.
      1. It is Maximum, not Minimum
      2. Impersonations are not Creatures, they are around 70% hollow, with the remainder creating a Sponge type effect inside of the Shell.
    4. The Creature is capable of increasing and decreasing its Density at Will, whilst only increasing in Mass when it Feeds on Memories, until it has completely subsumed more than 13 lifetimes worth of memories, at which point it creates a Spawn and reduces its Mass as well as its Density & Volume.
      1. (Density+) + (Mass=) = (Volume-)
      2. (Density-) + (Mass=) = (Volume+)
      3. (Density=) + (Mass-) = (Volume-)
      4. (Density=) + (Mass+) = (Volume+)
      5. (Density-) + (Mass+) = (Volume++)
      6. (Density+) + (Mass-) = (Volume--)
      7. (Density-) + (Mass-) + (Volume-) = (Offspring)
    5. You are also forgetting that the Creature would need to weigh more if it is a Huge, in addition to the Size increase.
      1. An Elder Oblex with the minimum number of Memories (3) only Weighs the minimum of its Size Category
        1. Large Minimum: 500 Lbs
        2. Huge Minimum: 2,000 Lbs
      2. Considering the Oblex Spawn is a Small, & the Oblex is a Medium, this would mean that an Oblex that has finally absorbed its 3rd victim's Memories would gain about 1,500 Lbs minimum in order to fill this quota.

    Most importantly though:

This means that it would be forced to devour what is quintessentially the memories of an entire Village just so that it is capable of being classified as an Elder Oblex so that it is capable of Reproduction, as the standard Oblex is incapable of splitting.
