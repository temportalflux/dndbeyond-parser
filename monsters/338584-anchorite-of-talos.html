Anchorite of Talos
Medium Humanoid (Half-Orc, Shapechanger), Neutral Evil
Armor Class 13 (hide armor)
Hit Points 58 (9d8 + 18)
Speed 30 ft.
STR
16 (+3)
DEX
13 (+1)

14 (+2)
INT
9 (-1)
WIS
15 (+2)
CHA
12 (+1)
Skills Nature +1, Stealth +3, Survival +4
Senses Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 12
Languages Common, Orc
Challenge 3 (700 XP)
Proficiency Bonus +2

Innate Spellcasting. The anchorite‚Äôs innate spellcasting ability is Wisdom (spell save DC 12). It can innately cast the following spells, requiring no material components:

1/day each: augury, bless, lightning bolt (8d6 damage), revivify

3/day: thunderwave (2d8 damage)

Shapechanger. The anchorite can use its action to polymorph into a boar or back into its true form, which is humanoid. Its statistics are the same in each form. Any equipment it is wearing or carrying isn‚Äôt transformed. It reverts to its true form if it dies.

Actions

Clawed Gauntlet (Humanoid Form Only). Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d4 + 3) slashing damage.

Tusk (Boar Form Only). Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d6 + 3) slashing damage.

Description

These religious recluses are granted spellcasting power by Talos, the god of storms. Their human ancestors bred with orcs, and now all anchorites of Talos are half-orcs.

    I am running Dragon of Icespire Peak and these are listed for the Woodland Manse quest.

    Supposedly three of these and six Boars and two Needle Blights per player are the exit encounter...

    The quest claims to be balanced for level 5-6 characters, but survivable for level 3-4 characters. I just tried setting it up in the encounter builder... With five level 6 characters and if only one of the nine Boars are Anchorites the encounter is "Hard", if two or three are the encounter is "Deadly".

     

    My group consisted of three level 4 characters, which had used all but one level 2 spell slot but were at full HP thanks to short rests.

    The experienced person in the party looked at me and exclaimed that something had to be wrong with the encounter after I had one of the Anchorites turn half-orc and lighting bolt them and the two other turn into half-orcs...

    And the player is right - how is a level anything (up to 6) party expected to survive three of these with all the adds if they had not just taken a long rest?

    We took a break and I changed two off the Anchorites to basic Boars, this gave the party the chance to hide in the manse and flee out of the kitchen entrance and into the woods...

    But I now face a similar issue with the counterattack described... 20 orcs and Gorthok the Thunder Boar are to attack Falcon's Hunting Lodge, where they are... Not sure what player-level equivalent Falcon is, but even with the players leveled up to level 5, I do not see that as a survivable encounter.

    Anyone else run this? How did you "fix" the encounters?
