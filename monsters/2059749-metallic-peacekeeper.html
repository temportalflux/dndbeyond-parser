Senses Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 11

Challenge 4 (1,100 XP)
Proficiency Bonus +2

Immutable Form. The peacekeeper is immune to any spell or effect that would alter its form.

Telepathic Bond. While the peacekeeper is on the same plane of existence as its master, it can magically convey what it senses to its master, and the two can communicate telepathically with each other.

Actions

Multiattack. The peacekeeper makes two Slam attacks.

Slam. Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 16 (3d8 + 3) bludgeoning damage.

Calming Mist (Recharge 5‚Äì6). The peacekeeper releases a calming gas in a 30-foot-radius sphere centered on itself. Each creature in that area must succeed on a DC 14 Charisma saving throw or become charmed by the peacekeeper for 1 minute. While charmed in this way, the creature is incapacitated and has a speed of 0.

Description

When a metallic dragon grows attached to a settlement of smaller folk‚Äîoften after dwelling there for a time in Humanoid form‚Äîthe dragon might decide to create a metallic peacekeeper to protect the place. The peacekeeper can ward a community for centuries, standing in the place of its dragon creator and maintaining peace and order.

     Your kinda ignoring a lot of variables that could easily cause a good party to need to fight these things:

    • They could be made by an evil (or opposed to the party) metallic dragon
    • The party could run afoul of authorities through petty crime or a political scheme 
    • These things are TYPICALLY good, that doesn't mean they can't be evil or corrupted

    At the end of the day, the good alignment is not a balancing factor. This thing is OP in a way that completely ruins the game. Roughly every 3 turns, it forces everyone within 30 ft to make a CHA save that most character's would reliably and consistently fail at (CHA saves aren't common so people don't pick the feat for them and maybe 4 or 5 classes out of 14 have proficiency in them). Upon failure, a creature is incapacitated for TEN TURNS, and likely has to make about 3 follow up saves in that time (because recharge) or have the timer reset back to 10 turns. On top of that, there is no subsequent saves for getting out early. Effectively, failing this means that a player gets kicked from the table until combat is over. 

    RAW this thing is awful, and the best case scenario for running it RAW is that players already know what it does and murder it before it gets a turn.
