    I'm seeing a lot of encounters in "Rime of the Frostmaiden" that are downright brutal to low-level adventurers...and I sort of dig it.

    If one manages to tastefully convey the imminent danger of some of these encounters...it gives the party a chance to adequately prepare, rather than be frustratingly overwhelmed.

    Take this...charming lass here: Maybe to emphasize just HOW deadly she is, have her use her "Wail" feature on a NPC...a random hunter, maybe...now, the party will see this a FREAK out, wondering if it will happen again if they approach.

    Being once per day, it won't...but THEY don't know that.

    Even then, if they choose to fight, it isn't going to be a stroll through the Lonelywood...but if the party manages to "destroy" the banshee...she'll just come back a day later.

    This will serve to emphasize a legitimate strategy not often used (at least not at my table): Sometimes it's better to stay away.

    For a horror-themed adventure...I can support that 

    I feel like I should low-key implant some kind of message somewhere in Ten-Towns that says "Remember: running away is a viable option."

As DM, if needed, you _technically_ can say the DC was lower if everyone fails. Or maybe a helpful hunter comes by and helps them. Or, maybe they just all die. Frostmaiden is a hard campaign.
