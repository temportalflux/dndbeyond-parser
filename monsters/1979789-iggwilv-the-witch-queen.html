In addition, there is a 50 percent chance that 3 hezrous then appear in unoccupied spaces in the sphere. They act as Iggwilv‚Äôs allies, take their turns immediately after hers, and can‚Äôt summon other demons. They remain until they die or until Iggwilv dismisses them as an action.

Spellcasting. Iggwilv casts one of the following spells, requiring no material components and using Intelligence as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 24, +16 to hit with spell attacks):

At will: detect magic, disguise self, invisibility, light, mage hand, message, prestidigitation, Tasha‚Äôs hideous laughter

3/day each: dispel magic, fly, polymorph

Bonus Actions

Fey Step. Iggwilv teleports, along with any equipment she is wearing or carrying, to an unoccupied space she can see within 30 feet of her.

Reactions

Negate Spell (2/Day). When Iggwilv sees a creature within 60 feet of her casting a spell, she tries to interrupt it. If the creature is casting a spell using a spell slot of 8th level or lower, its spell fails and has no effect. If it is casting a 9th-level spell, it must succeed on a DC 22 Intelligence saving throw, or the spells fails and has no effect.

Legendary Actions

Iggwilv can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature‚Äôs turn. Iggwilv regains spent legendary actions at the start of her turn.

Witchcraft. Iggwilv uses Spellcasting or Fey Step.

Dark Speech (Costs 2 Actions). Iggwilv utters a phrase in a forbidden language and targets one or two creatures she can see within 60 feet of her. Each target must succeed on a DC 22 Wisdom saving throw or take 11 (2d10) psychic damage and be frightened of Iggwilv for 1 minute. A target can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success and thereby becoming immune to Iggwilv‚Äôs Dark Speech for 24 hours.

Fey Beguilement (Costs 3 Actions). Iggwilv targets one creature she can see within 60 feet of her. The target must succeed on a DC 22 Charisma saving throw or be possessed by a fey spirit. While possessed, the target must obey Iggwilv‚Äôs commands. The target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, banishing the fey spirit and ending the effect on itself on a success.

Description

Iggwilv has a long and storied history throughout the multiverse. This adventure presents the legendary figure in her current form. 

Baba Yaga‚Äôs Daughter. Iggwilv‚Äôs path to greatness began when she was adopted by the mother of all witches, Baba Yaga, who named her Natasha. On the worlds of the Material Plane, she was better known as Tasha, the inventor of Tasha‚Äôs hideous laughter. Tasha‚Äôs magic-fueled ambitions brought her into contact with demons and demon lords, which she subjugated and used against her enemies. Tasha became known on the world of Oerth as Iggwilv the Witch Queen, a peerless wizard and conqueror. It was she who wrote the Demonomicon of Iggwilv‚Äîthe greatest of all treatises on the Abyss and its demonic inhabitants. (The book, which plays no part in this adventure, is described in Tasha‚Äôs Cauldron of Everything.)

Iggwilv‚Äôs Transformation. Iggwilv learned several dark secrets while studying the Abyss, including the ability to summon demons and demonic spirits. But in her quest for absolute power, she made too many enemies and underestimated their determination to destroy her, so she fled to other worlds and across planes to escape the worst of them. She eventually took a page from her mother‚Äôs book and sequestered herself in the Feywild, where she assumed the guise of an archfey and carved out a spectacular domain for herself. Gradually, Iggwilv transformed into a Fey creature, though she outwardly appears human. Her disposition changed over time as well, as she became less interested in personal power and more interested in exploring her fey existence

Roleplaying Notes

Iggwilv was a brilliant, calculating figure hardly prone to emotional outbursts, but as Zybilna, she allows her emotions to run rampant and is more likely to find herself in the grip of happiness, anger, or melancholy. This is the price she pays to exert control over the Feywild and impose her will on the domain of Prismeer.

    It is noteworthy that her Bewitching Bolt, Abyssal Rift, Fey Step, Negate Spell, Dark Speech and Fey Beguilement are all simply abilities that are distinctly separated from her spellcasting trait.  This means she is one of the few 'wizard' NPCs who is not immediately trivialized by Silence and Counterspell as there is no reason to infer these abilities have components.

    It is also worth noting that apart from Bewitching Bolt and Spellcasting none of her other abilities use the required terminology of 'magical' or 'casts a spell' n their description.  Compared to say the Paralyzing Touch or Disrupt Life of a Lich, or the Spellfire of Laeral Silverhand.  This would mean many of these abilities would function in an antimagic field per the very strict "rules as written" interpretation in Sage Advice.

    Joy of having stats for Tasha Aside, I love the new direction for spellcasting statblock. Giving them attack options that behave the same as cantrip/counterspell saves you from having to look up their spellcaster level or their CR (for the ones with innate spellcasting). It also helps lessen the need for counterspell wars, to an extent.

    Laying all spells as X/day like innate spellcasting/ daily powers, while a bit more annoying if you want to upcast something, is so much more streamlined and manageable for newer DMs. Just strike off the spells you used for the day and don't bother counting every spell slot anymore, everything else is written on the statblock. I can see it being a hit or miss depending on the DM, but it works for me, I much prefer giving more limited innate spellcasting to monsters and giving them some other unique abilities on the side.
