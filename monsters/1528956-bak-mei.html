    I would like to offer some feedback on this character. Perhaps you could call it a review as well.

    • I dig the theme and I love that he is a Pai Mei reference. In my opinion this is the most interesting "boss" encounter in the book.
    • It appears that his Staff of Striking is not correctly accounted for in his attacks, which seems like an oversight. His attack bonus should have been +12 and his damage dice should have a +7 bonus.
    • I find it unfortunate that this guy doesn't have Evasion, which is a pretty representative Monk ability that works well on creature stat blocks too.
    • This guy can stun like crazy, with a pretty high DC too. Three stuns per round can reduce a group of 5 to 2 active players. I feel like this guy could really control and stomp a group quite effectively. There also isn't a whole lot players can do to counter this, aside from having really solid CON saves and luck with dice.
    • He also has a staff that can punish a stunned creature extremely hard, since melee hits against a stunned opponent automatically crit and he can pool extra damage dice into a strike. Should be mentioned that he comes with minions who can punish a stun just as well.
    • Having a stun as a legendary action effectively enables this guy to remove a PC from a fight entirely (given unfortunate saves) while also chipping them down a good bit.

    Overall I would say this fight is interesting but might easily end up dramatically screwing the party over with just a few bad rolls. I would have placed a limit on his stunning ability. There is one stat block this could be compared to - a CR 16 Monk character from Waterdeep: Dragon Heist. That guy could stun up to 5 times per round, which is much more formidable than Bak Mei here, but his fists did a lot less damage and he couldn't punish a stun with a powerful magic weapon.
