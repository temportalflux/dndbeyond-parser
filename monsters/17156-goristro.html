Goristro

Goristro
Huge Fiend (Demon), Chaotic Evil
Armor Class 19 (natural armor)
Hit Points 310 (23d12 + 161)
Speed 40 ft.
STR
25 (+7)
DEX
11 (+0)
CON
25 (+7)
INT
6 (-2)
WIS
13 (+1)
CHA
14 (+2)
Saving Throws STR +13, DEX +6, CON +13, WIS +7
Skills Perception +7
Damage Resistances Cold, Fire, Lightning; Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing from Nonmagical Attacks
Damage Immunities Poison
Condition Immunities Poisoned
Senses Darkvision 120 ft., Passive Perception 17
Languages Abyssal
Challenge 17 (18,000 XP)
Proficiency Bonus +6

Charge. If the goristro moves at least 15 feet straight toward a target and then hits it with a gore attack on the same turn, the target takes an extra 38 (7d10) piercing damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 21 Strength saving throw or be pushed up to 20 feet away and knocked prone.

Labyrinthine Recall. The goristro can perfectly recall any path it has traveled.

Magic Resistance. The goristro has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

Siege Monster. The goristro deals double damage to objects and structures.

Actions

Multiattack. The goristro makes three attacks: two with its fists and one with its hoof.

Fist. Melee Weapon Attack: +13 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 20 (3d8 + 7) bludgeoning damage.

Hoof. Melee Weapon Attack: +13 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 23 (3d10 + 7) bludgeoning damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a DC 21 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.

Gore. Melee Weapon Attack: +13 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. Hit: 45 (7d10 + 7) piercing damage.

The goristro resembles a fiendish minotaur towering more than twenty feet tall. When controlled by a demon lord, goristros make formidable living siege engines and prized pets. Goristros possess preternatural cunning when navigating labyrinthine passages and shifting corridors, pursuing foes in a terrifying hunt.

A hulking goristro sometimes bears a palanquin, carrying smaller demons on its broad shoulders, much like an elephant carries riders on its back.

Monster Tags: Demon

Monster Manual, pg. 59

    Good notes here, much appreciated! I find that, in general, I can't afford to put my players up against solo monsters w/out Legendary actions and Lair actions and Legendary Resistances and the appropriate health and damage output. They're just too gamer-y, which I personally like. 

    If you happen to be like me an have one of these "crunchier" combat parties, as an alternate option, despite not being listed in the Monster Manual, let your Goristro boss monster use the demonic summoning variant. What I've started to do with creatures that have a recharge or something like that is roll it on Initiative Count 20 and narrate the results. I do the same thing for any creature with a summoning action. This gives your players the opportunity to vary up their strategies and especially their in-combat positioning. You can create space from your boss just by saying their lightning breath is ready to use on their next turn, and your players will start thinking about (or should start thinking about) spreading out to avoid the damage.  

    For example, in the case of your Goristro boss monster, you can make the Count 20 roll be the Percentile die that determines if he can summon his minions. You roll your 30% chance of summoning and succeed, so you say, "at Initiative Count 20, you see dark magic begin to roil off of Garkuvgaril as small tears begin to appear in the fabric of reality nearby. These fissures spew foul air and hateful, horrifying screams that are the stuff of nightmares. The demon's horrid lips curl into a sickening facsimile of a smile as he turns toward the fissures with a malevolent eagerness." 

    If you're using a battle map, mark 1 fissure per 2 players within 30 ft of the goristro. On his turn, he'll run over to the first one and rip it open and you can add a pair of dretches to the fight. He turns and looks at the other fissures and orders his minions to keep the PCs busy.

    Now instead of a "damage until he falls" fight, you have a secondary objective that has popped up in the middle of it. Killing Garkuvgarilthe Goristro is still priority 1 - but to do that, you're gonna need to not die to a demonic onslaught, so you might be able to eat up some action economy from your players to solve the summoning problem while still dealing a modicum of damage to them with your "adds." This way you can vary the damage being put out and the damage being applied to your boss, as well as mess up the action economy, without necessarily just giving your boss extra off-turn damage it can output.
