Abyssal Chicken
Tiny Fiend (Demon), Chaotic Evil
Armor Class 13 (natural armor)
Hit Points 10 (3d4 + 3)

Bad Flier. The abyssal chicken falls at the end of a turn if it‚Äôs airborne and the only thing holding it aloft is its flying speed.

Actions

Multiattack. The abyssal chicken makes two attacks: one with its bite and one with its claws.

Bite. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature. Hit: 4 (1d4 + 2) piercing damage.

Claws. Melee Weapon Attack: +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d6 + 2) slashing damage.

Abyssal chickens are carnivorous, temperamental, tasty bottom-feeders native to the Abyss. They beat their leathery wings to scare predators and to help them run faster, and they taste like fatty chicken.

With your permission, a character who casts the find familiar spell can henceforth choose to conjure an abyssal chicken instead of a raven.

Monster Tags: Demon

Baldur's Gate: Descent into Avernus, pg. 97

    I'm going reskin and rework these as my favorite Hearthstone monster: The Angry Chicken. I'm already running a D&D game where I cross the stream with D&D and Magic: The Gathering lore. So throwing a couple of Angry Chickens in the mix should be fun. 

    For those not familiar with Hearthstone, the Angry Chicken is a 1/1 creature that has "Enrage: this creature gains +4/+0 if it takes any damage." It's a very impractical creature, but it's great fun to pull off a combo where you boost your Angry chicken's health, ping it for one damage, and then attack the enemy hero with an enraged 5/1 chicken. I'll have to bump up the CR, but I will reskin this with a similar effect--that if it takes more than 1 damage and still lives, those attacks deal 5d4+2 and 5d6+2 damage, respectively. Funny as hell for me, but my players will hate me for it.
