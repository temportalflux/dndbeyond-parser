    Edit: To clarify: I'm not trying to say Aurelia isn't a good angel because she can't cast spells; i'm saying she doesn't possess any qualities that so far have made 5e angels, "angels". Divine like abilities like Divine Awareness, Healing touch, Shape Change, Telepathy,  innate spellcasting (to either aid or buff another creature or commune with their own god), etc. Angels are a bit more than "I swing sword and have radiant damage on top of that".

    Quote from STUV101 >>

    Hot take: This celestial is terrible and possesses very few qualities of celestial creatures in 5e.      I understand where you're coming from and I'm not trying to just pick a fight, but I think you're missing the point of who/what she is. You're right, she's not a celestial like most others in 5e, because that's not what she's meant to be. She is the leader of the Boros Legion of Ravnica, not the devoted of a god, not just some angel. I don't know how familiar with MTG Lore you are, but the Boros are basically the military of Ravnica. They are all about community, strength, and justice, and Aurelia is at the forefront of that ideology. 
    In the card game, Boros cards have a lot of abilities that care about attacking and specifically, attacking together. Her cards in the game give bonuses to creatures that attack with her, or give the other creatures additional attack steps. Her abilities with this stat block align perfectly with that. Her character isn't a spellcaster and isn't a healer. The only time she heals is with the Warleader's Helix card, which in contingent on dealing damage. (I think that ability is pretty well ported over to 5e rules.) You said she lacks flavor, but I vehemently disagree. She is DRIPPING in Ravnica specific lore and flavor. Maybe that doesn't do well in non-Ravnican campaigns, but she's a slam-dunk for Ravnica. You shouldn't view her as just another angel to play around with because that's not what she's intended to be.
